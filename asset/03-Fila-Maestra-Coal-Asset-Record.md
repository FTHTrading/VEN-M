**Operational Timeline:**
- **1978:** Mining operations began, operated by CAVOVEN
- **Engineering design:** British Coal Mining + E.P.M. (Estudios y Proyectos Mineros)
- **Peak production years:** 1987–1993 — cumulative output ~1,000,000 metric tons
- **1993:** Mine suspended due to global coal price decline + tectonic complexity
- **October 2017:** Venezuela's Minera Nacional conducted exploratory studies (Fila Maestra II and III)
- **April 2019:** Venezuelan government announced €9 million investment plan with Carbones Guri Guri for Fila Maestra II and III — reserve certification + preliminary operations
- **2022:** Geomatik Consultores, C.A. updated satellite imagery and GDEM analysis

## 5) Geologic Context

- Located in the **Zona de Corrimientos y Volcamientos** (thrust-and-fold belt) of the **Serranía del Interior Central**
- Tectonic complexity was a key challenge in 1978–1993 operation — engineering designs from British Coal Mining addressed extraction optimization
- Modern engineering and equipment would improve efficiency in this geological environment
- 2022 satellite imagery (Google Earth + Aster GDEM 1-arc-second) updated by Geomatik Consultores confirms deposit geometry

## 7) Market Context

- **Thermal coal demand:** Despite energy transition, thermal coal remains a significant commodity for emerging markets and electricity generation in South America and the Caribbean
- **Guyana export opportunity:** Guyana's rapid economic development (post-offshore oil discovery) drives energy demand — coal as baseload generation complement
- **Venezuelan domestic need:** Power sector deficit creates domestic demand for coal-fired generation feedstock
- **Historical validation:** ~1M MT produced 1978–1993 confirms the deposit is real and producible — suspension was economic, not geological

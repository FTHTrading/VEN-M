## 6) Events of Default

1. Failure to pay interest or principal when due (cure: 5 business days)
2. Breach of LTV maintenance covenant (cure: 30 days / margin call)
3. Insurance lapse (cure: 5 business days)
4. Loss or destruction of collateral without insurance recovery
5. Custody control agreement breach
6. Cross-default under any material project agreement
7. Bankruptcy / insolvency of Borrower or Guarantor (no cure)
8. Material misrepresentation in facility documents
9. XRPL attestation data tampering

**Lender remedies upon Event of Default:**
- Acceleration of all outstanding amounts
- Enforcement of custody control agreement (direct control of asset)
- Insurance claim collection
- Foreclosure on Venezuelan project interests
- UCC sale of Alexandrite collateral

## 7) Controls (Why This Is Bankable)

The CB Oriente portfolio is structured for institutional credit standards because:

1. **Hard collateral:** $42M appraised gemstone at conservative LTV — overcollateralized at even 50% LTV
2. **Evidence chain:** SHA-256 + IPFS + XRPL attestation = immutable, tamper-resistant proof of appraisal authenticity
3. **Independent custody:** Approved vault (Brinks/Malca-Amit/DMCC) + dual-control agreement = lender has enforcement rights
4. **Insurance:** $42M all-risk specie policy with lender as loss payee = insurance backstop against physical loss
5. **Cash flow basis:** Three operational projects provide >$9M EBITDA at Year 2 ramp = conservative debt service coverage
6. **Structured disbursement:** Milestone-based draws prevent capital misuse and align lender/borrower incentives
7. **Transparent operator:** US-domiciled beneficial owner (Florida) + complete diligence package

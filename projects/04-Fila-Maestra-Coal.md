## Executive Summary

The Fila Maestra coal deposit is a proven, government-documented thermal coal asset in the Anzoátegui Orinoco basin region of Venezuela. Three mining concessions — Fila Maestra I, II, and III — cover a deposit that produced nearly **1 million metric tons** of thermal coal between 1987 and 1993 before being suspended due to international coal price pressure (not geological limitations). As of 2022, all three concessions remain held, exploratory surveys have been completed, and the Venezuelan government itself announced a €9 million investment plan in 2019. The proposed restart targets **500,000 MT/year** of export-grade thermal coal, with Guyana as the primary export destination — aligned with Guyana's rapidly growing energy needs post-offshore oil discovery.

## Geologic Context

The deposit's tectonic complexity — located in the **Zona de Corrimientos y Volcamientos** (thrust-fold belt) of the **Serranía del Interior Central** — was a challenge for 1978-era operations. It was specifically addressed by the British Coal Mining + EPM engineering design, which "optimized the extraction process with minimum environmental impact."

Modern mining engineering, improved blasting precision, and contemporary geological modeling would address the same tectonic complexity more efficiently and safely, improving recovery rates and unit costs vs. the 1987–1993 operations.

## Market Context

### Guyana Export Opportunity

Guyana's offshore oil discovery (ExxonMobil-led Stabroek block) has triggered rapid economic development — GDP growth 60%+ in recent years. Baseload power generation is a critical constraint. Thermal coal remains a viable, lower-cost generation feedstock for emerging economy capacity additions.

### Venezuela Domestic Demand

Chronic electricity shortfalls in Venezuela require generation capacity additions. Coal-fired distributed generation represents a viable stopgap while PDVSA gas infrastructure recovers. Fila Maestra's proximity to Venezuela's central power grid (Anzoátegui) is an asset.

### Competitive Position

- **Proximity:** Shorter shipping distance to Guyana vs. Colombian or South African origin thermal coal
- **Existing infrastructure:** KM 235 coastal highway provides road access; port options in eastern Venezuela
- **Proven deposit:** ~1M MT historical production removes the exploration risk entirely — this is development, not exploration
